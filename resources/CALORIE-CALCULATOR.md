## How to Calculate Your Calories

### Step 1: Calculate Your BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate)

BMR is the calories your body burns at complete rest.

**Mifflin-St Jeor Formula (Most Accurate):**

**Men:**
```
BMR = (10 √ó weight in kg) + (6.25 √ó height in cm) - (5 √ó age in years) + 5
```

**Women:**
```
BMR = (10 √ó weight in kg) + (6.25 √ó height in cm) - (5 √ó age in years) - 161
```

### Step 2: Calculate Your TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure)

Multiply your BMR by your activity level:

| Activity Level | Multiplier | Description |
|----------------|------------|-------------|
| Sedentary | 1.2 | Desk job, little exercise |
| Lightly Active | 1.375 | Light exercise 1-3 days/week |
| Moderately Active | 1.55 | Moderate exercise 3-5 days/week |
| Very Active | 1.725 | Hard exercise 6-7 days/week |
| Extremely Active | 1.9 | Physical job + hard exercise |

**Your TDEE = BMR √ó Activity Multiplier**

### Step 3: Adjust for Your Goal

| Goal | Calorie Adjustment |
|------|-------------------|
| **Weight Loss** | TDEE - 500 calories |
| **Aggressive Weight Loss** | TDEE - 750 calories |
| **Maintenance** | TDEE (no change) |
| **Muscle Gain** | TDEE + 250-500 calories |

## Dubai Lifestyle Adjustments

### Ramadan Adjustments

During Ramadan, eating windows are compressed. Adjust by:
- Eating your full calories between Iftar and Suhoor
- Prioritizing protein at both meals
- Staying hydrated during eating hours
- Reducing training intensity if needed

### Summer Adjustments

Dubai summers require:
- Increased water intake (3-4+ liters)
- Slightly higher sodium intake
- Lighter meals during peak heat
- Morning or evening training only

### Social Event Planning

For brunches and dinners:
- Reduce calories earlier in the day
- Prioritize protein at the event
- Limit alcohol calories
- Return to normal eating the next day

## Common Questions

### How accurate is this calculator?

These formulas are estimates. Individual metabolism varies by:
- Muscle mass
- Genetics
- Hormones
- Sleep quality
- Stress levels

**For best results:** Use this as a starting point, then adjust based on actual results over 2-4 weeks.

### Should I eat back exercise calories?

**General rule:** No. The activity multiplier already accounts for exercise.

**Exception:** If you're doing significantly more exercise than your selected activity level, you may need to eat slightly more.

### Why am I not losing weight at my calculated calories?

Possible reasons:
- Underestimating food intake (most common)
- Overestimating activity level
- Metabolic adaptation
- Water retention
- Building muscle while losing fat

**Solution:** Track food accurately for 2 weeks, then adjust calories down by 100-200 if no progress.

### Is 1,200 calories safe?

**Generally no.** Very low calorie diets:
- Cause muscle loss
- Slow metabolism
- Are unsustainable
- Lead to nutrient deficiencies

**Minimum recommendations:**
- Women: 1,200-1,400 calories
- Men: 1,500-1,800 calories

If your calculated deficit puts you below these numbers, increase activity instead of cutting more calories.
